In the context of chiral (factorization) algebra, a “chiral groupoid action” is usually the structure that encodes the symmetries of a chiral category (or chiral algebra) by a groupoid in a way compatible with the chiral/ factorization operations, generalizing both ordinary group actions and chiral algebra actions on categories.[1][5]

### Basic idea

Informally, you have:

- A groupoid \(\mathcal{G} \rightrightarrows X\) (often something like a loop/jet groupoid over a curve \(X\)).[1]
- A chiral category \(\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{ch}}\) over \(X\) (e.g. the category of D-modules with the chiral pseudo-tensor structure, or the category of modules over a chiral algebra).[5][1]
- A “chiral action” of \(\mathcal{G}\) on \(\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{ch}}\) consists of functorial isomorphisms describing how arrows of \(\mathcal{G}\) transport objects of \(\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{ch}}\), compatible with:
- composition in the groupoid,
- units in the groupoid,
- and the chiral (factorization) composition operations in \(\mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{ch}}\) (pseudo-tensor / factorization structure).[5][1]

So, instead of an ordinary action functor \(\mathcal{G} \times \mathcal{C} \to \mathcal{C}\), you have a system of chiral operations
\[
P^{\mathrm{ch}}_I\big(\{ \text{“groupoid data at points in } I\},\, \text{objects of } \mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{ch}}\big) \to \text{objects of } \mathcal{C}^{\mathrm{ch}}
\]
satisfying associativity and unit axioms in the sense of pseudo-tensor (chiral) categories.[1]

### Relation to chiral operations

For a smooth curve \(X\), the category of D-modules \(M(X)\) carries a natural chiral pseudo-tensor structure via the spaces of chiral operations \(P_I^{\mathrm{ch}}(\{M_i\},N)\).[1]
A chiral action of a groupoid (or a group scheme, or a Lie* algebroid) is then encoded by requiring the relevant groupoid objects (or their enveloping chiral algebras) to act by compatible chiral operations on \(M(X)\) or its module categories.[5][1]

Concretely:

- Chiral operations form a pseudo-tensor structure \(M(X)^{\mathrm{ch}}\).[1]
- An action by a Lie* algebroid / groupoid over \(X\) is expressed by chiral operations that are compatible with the Leibniz rule and are polylinear with respect to the base algebra or groupoid.[1]
- These compatible chiral operations give a “chiral groupoid action” on the underlying category (e.g. D-modules, chiral modules).[5][1]

### Example schematic picture

As a schematic example, if \(\mathcal{G}\) is a groupoid of jets of automorphisms of a curve acting on chiral differential operators (CDOs), the action is not just pointwise but respects how local fields/operators “fuse” when points collide; this fusion is encoded by chiral operations, and compatibility of the \(\mathcal{G}\)-action with fusion is exactly the chiral groupoid action condition.[9][1]
